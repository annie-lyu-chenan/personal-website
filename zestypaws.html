How the Pet Parent Mindset Shape Supplements Perception?

  • #MarketingResearch

October - December 2025

*The project was developed in collaboration with Wenyue Zhang, Chun-Jung Hung, and Hexin Jing from Boston University College of Communication.

Research Objective

To explore how the emerging pet parent mindset shapes pet owners' health-related decisions and supplement purchasing behaviors.

Industry Overview

Pet health is a rapidly expanding market with strong demand.

  • The pet supplement category is growing consistently year-over-year.
  • Health-related products now make up a substantial portion of pet spending and is subjected for long-term growth.

Consumer Trends

The rise of the pet-as-child mindset is transforming how to make health decisions.

  • Emerging pet owners view pets as family members, increasing emotional involvement in care decisions.
  • Owners tend to focus on holistic wellbeing and long-term health.
  • Emotional engagement often occur during daily caregiving moments rather than only clinical triggers.

Consumer Personas

Pet parents differ in attachment mindset and supplement perception.

  • Attachment level predicts supplement orientation.
  • Owners' personal health philosophies spill over into pet routines.
  • Supplement choices are driven by emotional reassurance and professional validation.

Recommendations

Products and marketing strategies should be tailored to fit pet parents' caring routine and meet their cognitive needs.

  • Reframe supplements as part of a proactive, everyday health routine.
    • Use vet-backed claims to strengthen the value proposition for preventive care.
    • Embed supplements into daily wellness systems to drive habitual use.
    • Introduce integrated product ecosystems to shift consumers toward multi-supplement routines.
  • Tailor messaging and product entry points to the most opportunity-rich parent segments.
    • For high-attachment pet parents: Emphasize premium ingredients, evidence-backed efficacy, and emotional reassurance.
    • For emerging pet parents: Lower trial barriers with starter kits, trial sachets, simplified assortments, and guided onboarding.
  • Reduce decision friction through simplified choices and transparent education.
    • Rationalize product architecture to identify the right solution.
    • Provide interactive tools to guide selection with minimal cognitive load.
    • Offer transparent education like clean labels, clear benefits, and consistent claims to reduce confusion.
